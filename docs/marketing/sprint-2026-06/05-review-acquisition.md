# Review Acquisition Plan (compliant)

## Run a 14-day friends-and-family genuine-use review campaign (the core plan)
**Cost:** $0 · **Speed to first paid:** First ratings day 3-4; ratings unblock ASA conversion, so realistically 7-14 days to first paid via ASA traffic landing on a product page with stars · **Confidence:** high

WHO: 22-25 personal contacts, tagged by Apple Account storefront (have them check: App Store app -> tap profile photo -> country shows on the account row). Target mix: >=10 US-storefront (Vietnamese diaspora friends/relatives in the US, former colleagues, online friends), 2 each UK/CA/AU, remainder VN. Personal-ask conversion with one follow-up runs ~50-70%, so 22-25 asks yields 12-15 ratings. DAY-BY-DAY: Day 1 (Jun 7): build the tagged list; send Wave 1 (8-10 people, US-heavy) Template A. Day 2: support Wave 1 through onboarding + trial start; confirm each installed from the App Store, not TestFlight. Days 3-4: anyone who has genuinely logged meals for 2+ days gets Template B (review ask) — expect first 3-5 ratings; the in-app prompt may catch some before you even ask. Day 5: send Wave 2 (8-10 people incl. UK/CA/AU + VN) Template A; reply in App Store Connect to every review posted so far. Days 6-8: Wave 2 usage window; post try-my-app feedback threads on r/iosapps weekly thread and X #buildinpublic (ask for feedback, never for ratings, never offer anything). Days 9-11: Template B to Wave 2 — expect 4-6 more. Days 12-14: one polite follow-up to non-responders + 3-5 top-up asks. PACING RULE: never more than 2-3 new ratings per day per storefront. TEMPLATE A (EN, initial): "Hey [name] — I just launched my iPhone app, Voidpen. You take a photo of your meal and AI counts the calories. Could you try it for a few days and tell me honestly what's confusing or broken? There's a 3-day free trial — to make sure you're never charged, cancel right after subscribing (Settings -> your name -> Subscriptions -> Voidpen -> Cancel); you still keep all 3 days. Link: https://apps.apple.com/app/id6770921845 — one thing: install from the App Store, not TestFlight." TEMPLATE B (EN, after 2-3 days of real use): "Thanks so much for trying it — your feedback already helped. If you think it deserves it, an honest rating on the App Store would mean a lot right now (new apps with zero ratings basically don't get downloaded). Takes 20 seconds: https://apps.apple.com/app/id6770921845?action=write-review — whatever stars you think are fair, and a sentence in your own words if you have a minute. Totally fine if you'd rather not." TEMPLATE A (VI): "Chào [tên], mình vừa ra mắt app iPhone tên Voidpen — chụp ảnh món ăn là AI tự tính calo. Bạn dùng thử vài ngày rồi cho mình biết chỗ nào khó hiểu hay bị lỗi nhé? Có 3 ngày dùng thử miễn phí — để chắc chắn không bị trừ tiền, bạn hủy gia hạn ngay sau khi đăng ký (Cài đặt -> tên bạn -> Đăng ký -> Voidpen -> Hủy đăng ký), vẫn dùng đủ 3 ngày bình thường. Link: https://apps.apple.com/app/id6770921845 — lưu ý cài từ App Store chứ đừng cài bản TestFlight nha." TEMPLATE B (VI): "Cảm ơn bạn đã dùng thử nha! Nếu thấy app ổn, một đánh giá thật lòng trên App Store sẽ giúp mình rất nhiều lúc này (app mới 0 đánh giá gần như không ai dám tải). Chỉ mất 20 giây: https://apps.apple.com/app/id6770921845?action=write-review — bạn thấy mấy sao thì cho mấy sao, viết 1-2 câu theo lời của bạn càng tốt. Không tiện thì thôi cũng không sao!" CRITICAL RULES inside every ask: never dictate stars, text, or keywords; never compensate in any form; let them write in their own words (templated identical reviews are the #1 fraud signal).

*Evidence: Guideline 3.2.2(i) prohibits only 'paid, incentivized, filtered, or fake feedback' — genuine unpaid use + honest opinion is none of these (https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/). Apple's own ratings page encourages asking at moments of satisfaction (https://developer.apple.com/app-store/ratings-and-reviews/). The ?action=write-review deep link is Apple-documented marketing practice.*

## Concentrate ratings on the US storefront — Vietnam ratings are invisible to your ad traffic
**Cost:** $0 · **Speed to first paid:** Same timeline as the campaign; this targeting is what makes the ratings actually move ASA conversion · **Confidence:** high

Apple's summary rating and written reviews are strictly per-territory: 'This summary rating is specific to each territory on the App Store' (Apple, ratings-and-reviews page). A rating posts to the country of the reviewer's APPLE ACCOUNT, not their physical location — a VN Apple ID in California still posts to the Vietnam storefront. Since your $100 ASA credit will run in US/AU/CA/UK, every ad tap lands on those storefront pages: aim for 6-9 ratings on US (largest ASA volume share), 1-2 each on UK/CA/AU, and treat VN ratings as a bonus for VN organic only. Practical step: before sending Template B, confirm each contact's Apple Account country and log it in your tracking sheet so you know which storefront each rating will land on. Also: never check the 'reset summary rating' box on any future version release — at your volume it would wipe everything (Apple itself warns to use it sparingly).

*Evidence: Apple official: 'This summary rating is specific to each territory on the App Store and you can reset it when you release a new version' — https://developer.apple.com/app-store/ratings-and-reviews/; per-country review pools confirmed by https://appstorereview.app/guides/tracking-app-store-reviews-across-countries*

## Keep the existing in-app prompt trigger; fix one flag-handling flaw and verify on a production build
**Cost:** $0 (optional 3-line change) · **Speed to first paid:** Compounds from day 3 onward as friends and real ASA users hit the breakfast+lunch+dinner moment · **Confidence:** high

The current trigger in /Users/coduy/Documents/mobile_apps/voidpen/ios/calorietracker/Stores/ReviewPromptManager.swift (fire after breakfast + lunch + dinner all logged the same day, 0.7s delayed, once per install) is exactly Apple's recommended pattern — 'make the request when users... have completed an action, level, or task' — and it lands on trial day 1-2 while enthusiasm is highest, before the trial decision. Two config-level adjustments: (1) FLAW: hasRequested is set to true even when iOS silently declines to display the sheet (the system decides, max 3 displays per 365 days, and may show nothing) — so one missed display burns the prompt forever. Minimal fix: instead of a permanent boolean, store the date and allow one re-evaluation after 30 days (a 3-line change, not a redesign). (2) The prompt has NO EFFECT in TestFlight builds and shows-but-cannot-submit in development mode (Apple StoreKit docs), so verify the trigger fires on the App Store production build only — your sandbox testing will never have exercised the real path. Do NOT add any custom rating UI, star pickers, or 'enjoying the app?' pre-filter screens — 5.6.1: 'Use the provided API to prompt users to review your app... we will disallow custom review prompts', and sentiment pre-filtering is treated as 'filtered feedback' under 3.2.2(i). A passive user-initiated 'Rate Voidpen' row in Settings opening the write-review deep link is the only safe addition beyond the API.

*Evidence: Apple: prompt for ratings 'up to three times in a 365-day period', request at task-completion moments (https://developer.apple.com/app-store/ratings-and-reviews/); SKStoreReviewController docs — system controls display, no effect in TestFlight (https://developer.apple.com/documentation/storekit/skstorereviewcontroller); Guideline 5.6.1 disallows custom review prompts (https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/). Code verified at ReviewPromptManager.swift lines 18-36.*

## Migrate every TestFlight friend to the App Store build before asking for anything
**Cost:** $0 · **Speed to first paid:** 1-2 days to migrate; these are your warmest users, likely your first 2-3 ratings · **Confidence:** high

TestFlight users cannot generate App Store ratings, twice over: (1) the requestReview API is suppressed in TestFlight-distributed builds, and (2) a rating/review requires the Apple Account to have acquired the app from the App Store — a TestFlight install is not an acquisition, so the write-review action is unavailable to them. Action: message everyone currently on your TestFlight (check tester list via `asc` CLI), tell them to delete the beta, install from https://apps.apple.com/app/id6770921845, and go through the real onboarding + trial. Side benefit: they exercise the production purchase flow and show up as real RevenueCat trial events. Also note: anyone who redeems an App Store promo code for an app historically cannot leave a review on it — irrelevant for your free download, but never distribute subscription offer codes 'in exchange for' reviews (that converts a legal marketing tool into prohibited incentivized feedback).

*Evidence: Apple StoreKit documentation: requestReview 'has no effect when you call it in an app distributed using TestFlight' (https://developer.apple.com/documentation/storekit/skstorereviewcontroller); App Store review submission requires the app on the account — TestFlight overview (https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/test-a-beta-version/testflight-overview/)*

## Reply to every review within 24 hours from App Store Connect
**Cost:** $0, ~10 min/day · **Speed to first paid:** Indirect — protects the conversion value of every rating the campaign produces · **Confidence:** high

With under 20 reviews total, a developer response on every single one is cheap and visibly boosts trust for ASA visitors reading the page. Per 5.6.1: keep replies short, personal, respectful — no marketing language, no spam, no personal info. Format that works at your stage: thank them, reference the specific thing they mentioned, mention one upcoming improvement if relevant. Reviewers get notified of your reply and can edit their review upward. Prioritize anything below 4 stars or mentioning a bug — fix fast, reply that it's fixed, and the reviewer can update. Do this via App Store Connect -> App -> Ratings and Reviews, per storefront.

*Evidence: Guideline 5.6.1 response rules and Apple's response best practices: 'concise and clearly addresses your customer's feedback... no personal information, marketing language, or spam' (https://developer.apple.com/app-store/ratings-and-reviews/)*

## Hard DO-NOT list — each of these is a documented expulsion risk
**Cost:** $0 · **Speed to first paid:** Protective — an expulsion under 3.2.2(i)/5.6 ends the business entirely · **Confidence:** high

(1) NEVER pay or compensate for reviews in any form: cash, gift cards, free subscription months, offer codes tied to reviewing — 3.2.2(i) bans 'paid, incentivized' feedback even if you don't require it be positive. (2) NEVER join review-swap groups (Reddit/Discord/Telegram 'rate mine, I rate yours') — reciprocal feedback is incentivized feedback, and Apple's 5.6.3 (App Store Discovery Fraud) specifically calls out manipulation of charts, search, reviews, and referrals; repeated violations 'will lead to removal from the Apple Developer Program' (5.6). (3) NEVER buy from Fiverr/ASO 'guaranteed reviews' vendors — 3.2.2(i) explicitly names 'third-party services... on your behalf' and Apple has expelled developers for it. (4) NEVER self-review: no new Apple IDs, no VPN storefront-hopping, no rating from your own sandbox/test accounts — device/IP/account-age clustering is exactly what Apple's fraud systems detect. (5) NEVER gate features or the trial on rating ('rate to unlock') — 3.2.2(x): 'Apps must not force users to rate the app, review the app... in order to access functionality, content, or use of the app.' (6) NEVER build custom rating UI or 'happy? -> App Store / unhappy? -> email us' pre-filters — 5.6.1 disallows custom review prompts and sentiment-gating is 'filtered' feedback. (7) NEVER dictate review text, star counts, or ASO keywords to friends, and never have them paste a template — identical phrasing across new accounts in a burst is the canonical fraud signature. (8) NEVER exceed ~2-3 new ratings/day/storefront from your network — 10-15 over 14 days reads as a normal new-app curve; 15 in 48 hours from week-old accounts does not. (9) Don't reset the summary rating on updates. THE REAL RISK LINE: a friend who downloads free, genuinely uses the app for days, and writes their honest opinion in their own words is a real user — Apple cannot and does not distinguish them. Risk only appears with compensation, coordination at scale, zero-usage accounts, or templated text.

*Evidence: Guideline 3.2.2(i): 'paid, incentivized, filtered, or fake feedback... may include expelling you from the Apple Developer Program'; 3.2.2(x) forced-rating ban; 5.6 Developer Code of Conduct + 5.6.3 Discovery Fraud (https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/, https://developer.apple.com/news/?id=dovxb62h)*
